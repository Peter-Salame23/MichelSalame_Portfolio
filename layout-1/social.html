Overview

Michel Salameh's commitment to his community is written not in words, but in stone, infrastructure, and the lives that have been changed by lasting investment. His charitable works span the sacred and the practical — from a monument of faith that stands as a landmark of the world, to irrigation systems that have transformed agricultural livelihoods.

What sets Michel's approach apart is the permanence of what he builds. These are not one-time gestures or cheques quietly written. They are institutions, infrastructure, and sacred spaces that will serve generations — enduring testaments to the belief that a leader's greatest legacy is built in the communities he calls home.

What Was Built

01

Faith & Cultural Heritage

The World's Largest
Saint Charbel Statue

Michel Salameh spearheaded the initiative and secured the funding to erect the largest statue of Saint Charbel in the world — a monumental work of devotion and artistry that stands as a landmark of Lebanese faith and identity. The project brought together craftsmen, clergy, and community, and the statue now draws pilgrims and visitors from across the globe, placing the town on the international map of sacred destinations.

World Record Largest Saint Charbel Statue
02

Agricultural Development

$2M in US Treasury Funds for
Irrigation & Agriculture

Leveraging his diplomatic networks and political relationships, Michel successfully negotiated and secured $2,000,000 USD from the American Treasury Fund, directed toward irrigation infrastructure and agricultural development projects. The investment transformed the productive capacity of local farmland, providing farmers with reliable water access and modern agricultural support — securing livelihoods for hundreds of families.

$2,000,000 USD Secured from US Treasury Fund
03

Community Infrastructure

Municipality Building &
Public Clinic

Through a personal campaign of public fundraising, Michel raised the capital needed to construct two essential civic institutions for the town: a fully equipped municipal building to house local government and public administration, and a public medical clinic providing the community with accessible healthcare for the first time. Both buildings were funded entirely through private and community donations, built debt-free and handed over to the public.

2 Public Buildings — Funded by Community Donations
04

Public Works

$500K in Municipal
Machinery & Equipment

Recognising that a municipality without the tools to serve its people is a municipality in name only, Michel organised and donated approximately $500,000 USD worth of heavy equipment and machinery to the local municipality. The fleet — including construction vehicles, snow-removal machines, and public works equipment — equipped the town to maintain its roads, manage harsh winters, and carry out essential infrastructure works with full operational independence.
