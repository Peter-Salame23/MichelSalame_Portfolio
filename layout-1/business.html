Overview

Michel Salameh's entrepreneurial story is one of deliberate building — a portfolio assembled not for financial engineering, but for economic creation. Each venture has been chosen for its capacity to generate genuine value: jobs, infrastructure, and institutions that serve the communities in which they operate.

Spanning real estate development, professional services, and technology ventures, his business interests reflect the same clarity of purpose that has defined his legal and political careers. The enterprises he has built employ thousands directly and tens of thousands more in their supply chains.

Portfolio

Key Ventures

Company

Middle East Security

Michel is the owner of Middle East Security (MES), a leading security services firm founded in Lebanon and now operating across the region with offices in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. Recognized as the leading security company in Lebanon and ranked among the top 10 in the MENA region, MES delivers a comprehensive range of services including the secure transportation of cash and bonds on behalf of leading financial and banking institutions.

2k Employees

Professional Services

Salameh & Partners Law

One of the region's most respected law practices, specialising in constitutional litigation, commercial law, and arbitration. Founded on Michel's own legal legacy.

Properties

Real Estate Investments

Michel holds real estate across Canada, France, Greece, Côte d'Ivoire, and Lebanon — a diversified portfolio spanning four continents, balancing established markets with high-growth regions.

Hospitality

Hotels

Michel's portfolio extends into Beirut's hospitality sector with two luxury hotels currently under construction in the heart of the city. The first is scheduled to open in September 2026, with the second following in December 2026 — a significant investment in the revitalization of Beirut as a premier destination.

Philosophy
"The measure of an enterprise is not its profit margin — it is the number of families it sustains, and the communities it strengthens."

Michel's approach to business has always been shaped by his legal and political formation: an instinct for long-term structures, a respect for institutions, and a deep belief that private enterprise and public good are not in conflict — they are complementary.

He insists that every company he backs or builds must clear a second test alongside profitability: does it create lasting value for the people it employs and the communities it serves?
