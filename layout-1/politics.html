Chapter II

Political
Journey

"I entered politics not to be remembered, but to leave behind something worth remembering."

Overview

Michel Salameh's public career began in the newsrooms of Beirut and the corridors of Lebanon's government ministries — built on three decades of service in journalism, political counsel, and elected leadership. His path from political advisor to President of the Municipality of Faraya is marked by a consistent philosophy: that public life is a trust, and that trust is honoured through action, not words.

From his years advising Lebanon's Foreign Minister to hosting world-record diplomatic events and receiving some of the highest honours the Church and the Italian state can bestow, Michel's political journey has always been rooted in the communities he serves and the values he carries.

Chronology

Political Timeline

1992 – 1995
Media

Journalist — ICN & نداء الوطن (Nida2 Al-Watan)

Michel began his public career as a journalist, contributing to two of Lebanon's prominent media outlets — ICN and Nida2 Al-Watan (نداء الوطن). His work gave him a front-row seat to Lebanon's political landscape during one of its most turbulent periods, sharpening the analytical instincts and public voice that would define his later career in government and law.

1992 – 1995
Government Advisory

Political Advisor to the Foreign Minister of Lebanon

Concurrently with his journalism career, Michel served as a political advisor to Lebanon's Foreign Minister — a role that placed him at the centre of the country's international diplomacy during a defining chapter of its post-war history. His counsel spanned bilateral relations, regional negotiations, and the strategic positioning of Lebanon's foreign policy interests.

1995 – 1997
Government Advisory

Advisor — Ministry of Social Affairs & Independent Housing Fund

Michel served as advisor to the Ministry of Social Affairs and the Independent Housing Fund (وزارة الشؤون الاجتماعية والصندوق المستقل للإسكان), working on policy frameworks that addressed housing access and social welfare for Lebanon's most vulnerable citizens. His work in this role informed the community-first philosophy that has defined his subsequent public service.

2016 – 2025
Elected Office

Elected President of the Municipality of Faraya

In 2016, Michel was elected President of the Municipality of Faraya — a mandate of direct public trust that he has carried with the same rigour he brought to national advisory roles. Under his presidency, Faraya has seen landmark investments in public infrastructure, civic buildings, machinery, and community services. His leadership transformed the municipality into a model of efficient local governance in the Lebanese mountain region.

2017
International Recognition

Official Recognition in Italy for Municipal Leadership

Michel's work as President of the Municipality of Faraya earned him formal recognition from Italian institutions, awarded in acknowledgement of his contributions to municipal governance, community events, and the cultural exchanges he championed between Lebanon and Italy. The recognition placed Faraya on an international stage and affirmed Michel's standing as a model of enlightened local leadership.

2018
Honour

Patriarch Medal of Honor — Third Recipient in History

In 2018, Michel Salameh was awarded the Patriarch Medal of Honor — one of the most distinguished honours within the Lebanese Maronite tradition. He stands as only the third person in history to have received this medal, a testament to the depth of his service to the community, the Church, and the Lebanese people. The award recognised a lifetime of contribution to faith, civic life, and national leadership.

2018
Diplomacy

Host of Lebanon's Largest-Ever Ambassador Gathering

Michel organised and hosted a landmark diplomatic event that brought together the highest number of 28 ambassadors ever gathered at a single event in Lebanon's history — including the Vatican Ambassador. The gathering was a remarkable affirmation of his standing in the international diplomatic community and his ability to convene figures of global influence around shared values of dialogue, partnership, and peace.

2019
Papal Audience

Met & Received Blessing from Pope Francis

In 2019, Michel Salameh was received by Pope Francis and received his personal blessing — a profound moment that reflected both his standing in the international Catholic community and the depth of his faith. The audience with the Holy Father stood as one of the most personally meaningful milestones of Michel's public life, and a recognition of his commitment to values of service, community, and peace.
